Why DCA Helped

Dollar-Cost Averaging saved you. By buying Bitcoin every month (not all at once), you:

  • Bought Bitcoin at $47K (Jan 2022), $16K (Dec 2022), and $104K (Jan 2025)
  • Your average cost: ~$42K vs. market peak of $69K
  • Avoided the worst-case of buying all at the 2021 top
  • Captured the 2023-2024 recovery (Bitcoin up 155% from lows)

Should You Add Bitcoin Now?

The data is real, but timing matters. Starting in 2022 (near the top) was painful but DCA helped. Starting today at $104K? Different risk/reward. Consider:

  • Start tiny: 1-2%, not 5% - test the water first
  • Use DCA: Monthly purchases, not lump sum
  • Board tolerance: Can they handle a 50% drawdown in this allocation?
  • Alternatives bucket: Replace some traditional alts, don't add to risk
  • Trend: 12% of endowments/foundations now hold 1-5% crypto

The Honest Verdict

An extra $9.5M over 3 years is substantial - that's real money that could fund more grants. Yes, you endured a -6.8% portfolio return in 2022 (vs -2.3% without Bitcoin), but the subsequent recovery more than made up for it. Bitcoin's 3-year DCA return (+95%) demonstrates that monthly buying through a crash can work spectacularly well. For foundations willing to stomach higher volatility (18% vs 10%), a small allocation delivered meaningful outperformance. The question isn't "should we have done this?" (clearly yes), but "should we start now at $104K Bitcoin?" That's a different risk/reward calculation.
